Biography

I am a computer architecture research scientist at Hewlett Packard Labs. My current research focuses on interconnection networks for large-scale high-performance computing (HPC) systems. I am designing, analyzing, simulating, and implementing low-cost high-performance network architectures enabled by photonic interconnects.

My Ph.D. thesis was on the design of a high performance distributed system architecture for secure service-oriented computing aiming to bridge the performance and security gap between supercomputing and cloud computing. Prior academic projects include arbitration structures for high-radix routers, attacks on SSL and the public key infrastructure, precise time synchronization of 804.15.4 wireless networks, an RTL architecture and implementation for accelerator multithreading, stabilization systems for autonomous helicopters, and a from stratch 32-bit RISC ISA and accompanying RTL processor implementation.

Prior to working at Hewlett Packard Enterprise, I worked in Google’s platform networking group designing next generation data center network topologies and congestion control and load-balancing algorithms. Prior to that I worked at L-3 Communications designing and implementing communication systems for UAVs and other military devices.
